My Assam Adventure

Recently i visit Assam Tea Garden.It is a beautiful place for travalers and it is a green garden which is look like very beautiful.Here, tradition thrives amidst the gentle hum of production, creating the world's finest cup of Assam tea.

  1. Assam is the world's largest tea-growing region, and the tea gardens are located in the Brahmaputra Valley in northeastern India. The climate there is ideal for growing tea, with cool, arid winters and hot, humid rainy seasons
  2. The Assam tea region produces close to 507 million kilograms of tea every year, making it the most lucrative tea region in the world. There are two main types of tea produced in Assam: Orthodox and CTC (Crush, Tear, Curl).
  3. Assam is the birthplace of the Indian tea industry. The first tea plant in India was discovered in Assam in the early 1820s, and the first commercial tea production began there in the 1830s.
  4. The tea industry is a major source of income for Assam. It employs a large number of people in the state, both directly and indirectly.

The Punakha in Bhutan

Recently i go to visit Punakha in Bhutan.Punakha Dzong, also known as Punakha Temple, isn't just a temple - it's a massive fortress-monastery! Located in Bhutan's winter capital, Punakha, this architectural wonder is nicknamed "Palace of Great Happiness" and offers stunning views alongside religious significance.

  1. Punakha Dzong is a unique building that combines a fortress (dzong) and a monastery. It has been the winter home for Bhutan's central monk body for centuries.
  2. The name "Punakha Dzong" translates to "Palace of Great Happiness," reflecting its grand architecture and spiritual significance.
  3. Punakha Dzong is a beautiful example of Bhutanese architecture, with whitewashed walls, elaborate woodwork, and colorful prayer flags.
  4. Punakha served as the capital of Bhutan during the winter months until the 1950s. It remains an important cultural and religious center.
  5. The most prominent landmark in Punakha is the magnificent Punakha Dzong, a fortress-monastery that is considered one of the most important Dzongs in Bhutan.
